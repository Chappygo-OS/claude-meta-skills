# Before / After Transformation Examples

Used by `/humanizer`. Reference examples showing how AI writing transforms into human writing.

---

## Example 1: LinkedIn Post (Business Topic)

**Before (AI)**:
> In today's rapidly evolving business landscape, leveraging artificial intelligence has become a game-changer for companies looking to unlock their full potential. Many businesses are now using cutting-edge AI tools to transform their workflows and achieve incredible results. It's worth noting that this transformative technology can help you dive deeper into your data and gain actionable insights that drive meaningful outcomes.
>
> Here are three key ways AI is revolutionizing business:
> 1. It automates repetitive tasks
> 2. It provides data-driven insights
> 3. It enhances customer experiences
>
> The bottom line is that companies that fail to adopt AI will be left behind. At the end of the day, the question is not whether to use AI, but how to use it effectively.

**After (humanized)**:
> We cut our proposal writing time from 4 hours to 45 minutes last quarter.
>
> Not because we hired faster writers. Because we stopped writing proposals from scratch.
>
> Here's the actual workflow now: a call ends, I dump my notes into Claude, and a structured first draft is ready before I've closed the Zoom window. I spend 20 minutes editing, not 4 hours staring at a blank doc.
>
> Three things made this work:
> — A prompt template built from our 50 best-performing proposals
> — A review checklist so nothing important gets missed
> — A hard rule: the AI draft is a starting point, never the final version
>
> The proposals are better. Response rates went up 18%. And I get Friday afternoons back.

**What changed**:
- Replaced generic landscape intro with a specific concrete result (4 hrs → 45 min)
- Removed all hype words (game-changer, transformative, cutting-edge)
- Turned symmetrical bullet list into an uneven, real workflow
- Added specific numbers (18% response rate)
- Removed "bottom line" and "at the end of the day" closes
- Made it first-person and specific instead of generic and universal

## Example 2: Email — Client Proposal Intro

**Before (AI)**:
> I hope this email finds you well. I am reaching out to follow up on our recent conversation regarding the exciting opportunity to collaborate on your upcoming project. I am incredibly excited about the possibility of working together and believe our extensive experience and cutting-edge expertise will be of great value to your organization.
>
> Please find attached our comprehensive proposal, which outlines our innovative approach and the transformative results we can help you achieve. We are confident that our holistic methodology will seamlessly integrate with your existing workflows.

**After (humanized)**:
> Attached is the proposal we discussed on Tuesday.
>
> It covers the three phases we talked through: the audit in week 1–2, the rebuild in weeks 3–8, and the handover sprint in weeks 9–10. Timeline and pricing are on page 4.
>
> One thing I added that wasn't in the brief: a risk section on page 7. Given the legacy system you're migrating from, there are two edge cases worth discussing before we sign. Happy to walk through them on a call.

**What changed**:
- Deleted "I hope this email finds you well" opener
- Removed: excited, cutting-edge, innovative, transformative, holistic, seamlessly
- Made subject concrete (three specific phases with real timeline)
- Added genuine value (risk section they didn't ask for) instead of generic confidence claims
- Ended with a specific, actionable offer instead of vague enthusiasm

## Example 3: Newsletter Section (Tech Topic)

**Before (AI)**:
> Furthermore, it is worth noting that the rapidly evolving landscape of large language models has created incredible opportunities for businesses and individuals alike. As we know, these powerful tools have become increasingly sophisticated, allowing users to leverage their capabilities in ways that were previously unimaginable. Moreover, the seamless integration of AI into various workflows has transformed how many companies operate.
>
> To summarize, AI is no longer a futuristic concept but a present reality that can drive meaningful results.

**After (humanized)**:
> Six months ago, the best open-source model couldn't write production code. Now it can.
>
> Llama 3.1 70B passed a benchmark I care about: it completed a real refactor task in our codebase without breaking tests. GPT-4 couldn't do that reliably 12 months ago.
>
> What changed wasn't the model size — it was how the model was trained to reason about code. The 2025 open-source models aren't just bigger. They think differently about structured tasks.
>
> That matters if you're an engineering team that dismissed open-source as "good enough for demos." It's not anymore.

**What changed**:
- Deleted "Furthermore", "Moreover", "As we know", "To summarize" entirely
- Removed: rapidly evolving landscape, incredible, increasingly sophisticated, leverage, seamlessly, meaningful results
- Replaced vague "AI is improving" with a specific concrete example (Llama 3.1 70B, a specific benchmark, a real test)
- Added a specific time reference (6 months ago, 12 months ago)
- Ended with a direct, specific implication for the reader instead of a generic "AI is here" conclusion

## The Core Pattern

In every example above, the transformation follows the same rule:

**Generic → Specific**
**Vague → Concrete**
**Claimed → Proven**
**Announced → Shown**

The question to ask about any sentence: "Can I back this up with a specific example, number, or story?" If yes, add it. If no, cut it or soften the claim.
