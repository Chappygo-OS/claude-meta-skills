# Question Banks by Process Type

Used by `/process-interviewer`. After the 10 core questions, pull 3–5 additional questions from the relevant category below.

---

## Writing & Content Creation

For skills that produce written output (posts, emails, newsletters, proposals, scripts).

1. Who is the reader — specifically? (Title, company type, relationship to you)
2. What do you want the reader to feel or do after reading this?
3. What's the tone? (Formal/casual, expert/accessible, warm/direct)
4. What do you always include that's non-negotiable? (Calls to action, disclaimers, section headers)
5. What makes you immediately delete a draft? (Specific things you never want to see)
6. How long should the output typically be?
7. Do you have examples of past pieces that hit the mark? Can you share one?
8. Is there a structural template you always follow, or does it vary?
9. Will you edit the output, or do you want it ready to publish as-is?
10. How will the input arrive? (Bullet points, voice note, transcript, brief, free text?)

---

## Research & Analysis

For skills that gather, process, or synthesize information.

1. What specific questions does the research need to answer?
2. What sources should it prioritize? (Web, internal documents, specific databases)
3. What sources should it avoid or deprioritize?
4. How deep should it go — breadth vs. depth?
5. Who will read the output, and what will they do with it?
6. What format works best for you: bullets, narrative, table, scored comparison?
7. What does "complete" look like vs. "good enough to move forward"?
8. How often will you run this? (One-off vs. recurring)
9. Is there a specific time frame or scope for the research?
10. What should it flag as uncertain or unverified?

---

## Communication & Email

For skills that draft, respond to, or process communication.

1. What's the relationship with the person you're communicating with? (Client, prospect, colleague, boss)
2. What action or response do you want from them?
3. What's the right tone — how formal? How warm?
4. Are there things you never say in professional communication? (Specific words or phrases to avoid)
5. Do you have a signature, standard sign-off, or boilerplate you always include?
6. How long should responses typically be?
7. Will this go out directly, or will you review and edit first?
8. What context do you typically need about the sender before drafting a response?
9. Are there common scenarios this needs to handle? (Follow-ups, rejections, updates, requests)
10. What's the biggest mistake an AI makes when drafting emails for you?

---

## Operational & Workflow Processes

For skills that automate or streamline internal operations.

1. What system or tool does this process live in today?
2. What are the triggers — what starts this process?
3. What are the outputs — where do results go?
4. How many exceptions or special cases exist, roughly?
5. Who else touches this process, and where does it hand off to them?
6. What's the cost of the process going wrong? (Risk level)
7. What does "done" look like — what does a completed output look like?
8. Are there regulatory, compliance, or legal constraints I should know about?
9. What approvals are required before an output is final?
10. What parts of this could NOT be automated, even in an ideal world?

---

## Data Processing & Analysis

For skills that transform, extract, or analyze structured or unstructured data.

1. What format does the input data arrive in? (CSV, JSON, PDF, spreadsheet, paste)
2. What's the output format? (Table, report, summary, JSON, chart)
3. How large is a typical data set? (Rows, pages, files)
4. What are the key fields or columns that matter most?
5. What transformations need to happen? (Filter, aggregate, normalize, merge)
6. How should errors or missing values be handled?
7. Is there a schema or template the output must match?
8. Who consumes the output, and how? (Manual review, automatic import, presentation)
9. What are the most common data quality issues you encounter?
10. Should it explain its reasoning, or just deliver the result?

---

## Skill / Automation Building

For skills that produce other skills, workflows, or automations.

1. What tool or platform will this skill run on? (Claude Code, Claude.ai, Zapier, Make, etc.)
2. Who will use this skill — just you, or a team?
3. How technical are the end users?
4. What skills or automations have you built before that worked well?
5. What have you built before that failed — and why?
6. What integrations does this need? (Specific tools it connects to)
7. What should Claude do when it's uncertain vs. when it should just proceed?
8. Should the skill ask clarifying questions, or assume and act?
9. How much should the output explain its reasoning vs. just delivering the result?
10. What's the "definition of done" — what would make you consider this skill finished?

---

## Decision Support

For skills that help analyze or frame decisions.

1. What type of decisions is this for? (Pricing, hiring, product, partnerships — be specific)
2. What information do you typically have when making this decision?
3. What information do you wish you had?
4. Who else is involved in the decision?
5. How much time do you typically have to decide?
6. What frameworks or mental models do you already use?
7. Do you want the skill to give a recommendation, or just structure the analysis?
8. What's the biggest mistake you've made in this type of decision in the past?
9. What would make you trust the skill's output vs. override it?
10. Should the output be a document, a structured list, or a conversational dialogue?
